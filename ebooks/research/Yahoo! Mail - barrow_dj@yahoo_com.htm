Hello,

Here are some of the links we discussed for nuclear thermal rockets 
(NTR) 
papers that can give you some ideas for your writing. :)  We usually do 
not 
consider firing the NTR for the launch from the Earth, due to safety 
considerations.  Hope that these references help.


TITLE AND SUBTITLE:
Vehicle and Mission Design Options for the Human Exploration of 
Mars/Phobos 
Using "Bimodal" NTR and LANTR Propulsion

AUTHOR(S):
Stanley K. Borowski, Leonard A. Dudzinski, and Melissa L. McGuire

http://gltrs.grc.nasa.gov/reports/2002/TM-1998-208834-REV1.pdf

and

TITLE AND SUBTITLE:
High Power Nuclear Electric Propulsion (NEP) for Cargo and Propellant 
Transfer Missions in Cislunar Space

AUTHOR(S):
Robert D. Falck and Stanley K. Borowski

http://gltrs.grc.nasa.gov/reports/2003/TM-2003-212227.pdf

You can also do a general search at:

http://gltrs.grc.nasa.gov/ask.htm


Atomic rockets (using chemical recombination) are different, having no 
radiation. :)

See:

Launch Vehicle Performance for Bipropellant Propulsion Using Atomic 
Propellants With Oxygen
http://gltrs.grc.nasa.gov/reports/2000/TM-2000-209443.pdf

See you,

Bryan
