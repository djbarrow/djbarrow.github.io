Subject: Re: seeing as Jupiter has a suitable atmosphere for an atomic ramjet
Hello,

Physics of going at light speed QUITE is difficult. :)   If you do some 
calculations, you'll find that going to the nearest star in a 40 to 45 
year 
time frame (about 1/10th of the speed of light) requires more than the 
total WORLD (i.e. Earth's) power output.  Depending on the technology 
assumptions, it can be between 1 and 100 times the WORLD'S power 
output.  Thus, we go to Jupiter and mine it's atmosphere for the fuel.

If you fly at high speed (beyond orbital velocities) in an atmosphere, 
aircraft tend to melt, unless they use extreme measures for thermal 
protection.  The temperatures of the aircraft skin can reach amazing 
levels. :)  Hence we use ablative heat shield when spaceship return 
from 
the Moon, etc.  Those vehicles have with initial velocities entering 
the 
atmosphere of 25,000 MPH, and due to drag, they slow down quickly.

So, travelling at VERY high speed in the atmosphere, let alone the 
speed of 
light, is rather impractical.  :)  This is a BIG understatement.

See you,

Bryan
