Hello,

Just so we are clear, you've misinterpreted my statements (or lack of 
statements) in your draft.  We cannot get humans to Mars or to the 
nearest 
star with existing technology.  The NTR work will require many years of 
development, and the Daedalus Project was a paper study.

For Daedalus, we'd need massive robotic factories to assemble the 
vehicle, 
which weighs 50,000 metric tonnes when it departs Jupiter.  Acquiring 
the 
fuel from Jupiter's atmosphere (of about 30,000 to 40,000 metric 
tonnes) 
takes 20 years of massive robotic factories floating in the 
atmosphere.  For Daedalus, none of these capabilities exist, or are 
likely 
to exist for a LONG LONG time.

 From your draft:

Excerpt:

He also found nothing obviously impossible in my proposal, a mission to
Mars & even Alpha Centauri is very feasible using todays technology. 
This

End excerpt:


All of these things (whether it be a human mission to Mars and any 
mission, 
robotic or human, to Alpha Centauri) are not "been there, done that" or 
"very feasible with today's technology."  Please don't say that in your 
book, as it is not true.

Have a great day.

See you,

Bryan
